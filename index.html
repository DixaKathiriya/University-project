About Ralph Ross

My name is Ralph E. Ross Sr., and I am a longtime resident of Washington Township, where all four of my children attended and graduated from.
I am a graduate of the “College of New Jersey” with a B.A. and M.A. degree. I also have administrative course work for Rutgers and Rowan University.
I have served as a teacher, supervisor, principal, assistant superintendent, and superintendent of schools for the Black Horse Pike Regional School District, Deptford School District, and Monroe School District. ...

Read more

I have also been involved in educating students all of my adult life, having served as a member of this school board, and as president during the rebuilding and transformation of many of the buildings and changes that we are fortunate to use now.
I was Chairman of the Gloucester County College Board of Trustees, Chairman of the New Jersey Interscholastic Athletic Associations, and served as a member and chairman of many middle state evaluations throughout New Jersey.
I have coached little league baseball and football for the WTAA, and coached high school football and wrestling at Highland Regional High School.
I am running for this School Board because I believe that we are living in a technological society with the emphasis on tools, materials, and ideas where it is necessary that we educate for the future. Our students must be prepared and educated for these changes, whether they are college bound or entering the trades.
I believe with my experience as an educator, we will be able to give our administrators, teachers, and staff the necessary resources they need to accomplish our mission.
